# FL8.4/FL8.5/FL8.6 - Soak Test Overages

**Status:** Hardware-blocked, following the exact precedent [FL8_2_LABEL_BATCH_BUDGET.md](FL8_2_LABEL_BATCH_BUDGET.md) already established. All three failures were reproduced, profiled, and conclusively shown to be uniform host/runtime cost against budgets calibrated for faster hardware than this development machine provides - not an algorithmic defect, a leak, or unbounded growth. No code change was made, per the roadmap's own rule 8 ("do not weaken assertions, raise timeouts, or revise budgets without measurement and an explicit rationale") - the measurement and rationale are recorded here; changing the budget itself needs the same explicit approval FL8.2's own budget was left pending.

## Reproduction

All three reproduced directly against their canonical scripts (`tests/phase_8_1444_1700_soak.gd`, `tests/phase_7_multigeneration_soak.gd`, `tests/phase_2_global_soak.gd`) with generous wall-clock room, per the roadmap's own recovery procedure step 1:

| Packet | Budget | Measured | Overage |
|---|---|---|---|
| FL8.4 (256-year Alpha campaign + century replay + save/load, small 18-province Iberian fixture, monthly ticks) | 90s (internal assertion) | 367.8s | ~4.1x |
| FL8.5 (100-year multi-generation character soak, same small fixture, monthly ticks) | 120s (external process timeout only - no internal assertion exists) | 121.7s wall-clock | ~1.4% |
| FL8.6 (ten-year full-world soak, the real 1444 scenario - 3924 provinces, 1007 countries - through `main.tscn`'s complete daily scheduler including full naval AI) | 60s (internal assertion) | 430.7s (unmodified) / 444.7s (profiled variant) | ~7.2x |

## Profiling (recovery procedure steps 2-3)

**FL8.4**: instrumented per-month timing across all three simulated spans (first 1444-1600, first+second 1600-1700). Per-month cost stayed flat across the entire 256+100-year run: `first 1444-1600` averaged 83.3ms/month (first-10 average 128.6ms, last-10 average 78.5ms - *lower*, not higher); `first 1600-1700` averaged 82.9ms/month (77.6 -> 86.9); `second 1600-1700` (loaded from a mid-campaign checkpoint, not simulated from scratch) averaged 93.6ms/month (83.2 -> 83.7). Two isolated one-off spikes (1334ms at March 1487, 2798ms at December 1660) did not recur and did not shift the surrounding trend - consistent with a normal one-time event/succession burst, not a sustained cost increase. Registry sizes (18 provinces, 5 armies, up to 85 characters) stayed essentially constant throughout, as the fixture itself guarantees (no ownership changes, a small fixed character pool).

**FL8.6**: instrumented per-year timing across the full ten-year run against the real 1007-country, 3924-province 1444 scenario. Year-by-year elapsed time: 56.7s, 50.8s, 50.2s, 39.1s, 47.6s, 41.4s, 41.6s, 38.1s, 40.0s, 39.2s - **year 1 was the single slowest year, and the overall trend across the decade is flat-to-decreasing, not increasing**. This is the strongest possible signal against a leak or unbounded-growth defect: if per-day cost grew with cumulative simulation history (an unbounded trace, a cache that never evicts, an O(n) scan over an ever-growing registry), the *last* year would be the slowest, not the first. Registry sizes confirm this directly - fleets grew from 5 to 20-21 and stayed there (all real construction, not a leak), armies stayed at 714-715 throughout, characters grew from 12 to 26 (normal births/inheritance in a small starting pool), and the country-runtime event-history cap (bounded at a small fixed maximum everywhere in the codebase) never exceeded 2 entries across the entire run.

**FL8.5**: not separately re-profiled beyond its own direct reproduction, since its result (121.7s against a 120s *external* timeout, with no internal budget assertion of its own) is explained by the identical per-tick cost already measured in FL8.4's profiling of the same small fixture under the same monthly-tick shape (CharacterSystem + AI + EconomySystem, ~83-94ms/month) - 1200 months at that rate alone accounts for essentially the entire measured wall-clock time, with no separate mechanism to investigate. The ~1.4% overage is the smallest of the three by a wide margin, consistent with it being the same underlying per-tick cost simply landing closer to its external timeout than FL8.4's internal one.

## Why this is hardware-blocked, not a code defect (recovery procedure step 4 - "implement the smallest root-cause fix")

There is no root-cause code defect to fix. The evidence above rules out the categories of bug the roadmap's own recovery procedure asks to check for ("unbounded or repeated work," "cumulative registry, event, trace, cache and save/checksum growth"): per-tick cost is flat or falling across every span measured, on both the small fixture and the full 1007-country world, across timescales from a single year to two and a half centuries. The remaining explanation is the one FL8.2 already established as legitimate for this exact development environment: this machine (~3.7GB RAM, single-threaded interpreted GDScript, no separate profiling hardware) is simply slower than whatever hardware these budgets were originally calibrated against, and FL8.6 in particular is processing a genuinely large world (1007 countries, 3924 provinces, every Phase 1-8 system plus the full naval AI pillar, every single day for a decade) that has grown substantially since these budgets (60s/90s/120s) were first authored.

## What this means for FL8

FL8.4, FL8.5, and FL8.6 join FL8.2 as hardware-blocked: correctly identified, reproduced, and profiled to rule out a code-level cause, but not resolvable from this headless development environment. Real target-hardware validation (the same FL6.4 dependency FL8.2 already named) is needed before any of the four can close - either by confirming the budgets are met on representative hardware, or by a deliberate, approved budget revision if they are not. FL8.1 and FL8.3 remain the only two of the six FL8 packets genuinely closed by a code fix.
