# FL3.6 - Explainability and Bounds

**Status:** Complete for every field the closure audit named as genuinely missing. Targeted tests pass (see Verification).
**Satisfies:** the FL3.6 findings recorded in [FL3_CLOSURE_AUDIT.md](FL3_CLOSURE_AUDIT.md) - "record country, day, posture, action, targets, selected score, major rejected candidates, constraints and next planning day" per decision, plus the missing "candidates evaluated" counter. `country` was already correctly judged implicit by the audit and stays that way; every other named field is now a real, structured entry rather than free text or a separately-tracked value.

## What shipped

`_record_decision()` (`naval_ai_system.gd`) now takes two new optional parameters, `targets: Array` and `constraints: Dictionary`, threaded through every one of `NavalAISystem`'s decision points - the twelve `_consider_*`/`_plan_*` functions and every `_submit()` call - with real values, not placeholders:

- **`targets`** - the entity IDs (fleet, province/port, zone, battle, army, admiral) a specific decision actually concerned. A retreat decision names the fleet and the battle it withdrew from; a construction decision names the port; a home-port reassignment names the fleet and its new port; a transport decision names the army, the fleet, and the landing province. Country-wide bookkeeping decisions (posture review, "fleet_sufficient," "hold_stations") correctly carry an empty `targets` array rather than a fabricated one.
- **`constraints`** - the actual numeric bound, ratio, or threshold value that gated the branch taken, by name: `_review_posture()` records `treasury`/`reserve`/`at_war`/`in_debt`; `_consider_retreat()` records `own_power`/`enemy_power`/`retreat_ratio_bp`; `_consider_blockade_or_evade()`'s evade branch records `threat`/`own_power`/`evade_ratio_bp`; construction's sailor/reserve rejections record the exact sailor cost and reserve numbers compared. Not every decision has a meaningful constraint (e.g. "assign a free admiral" has no threshold to name) - those correctly carry an empty `constraints` dictionary rather than an invented one.
- **`posture`** - copied from the country's own current `state["posture"]` into every decision record, not left as a separate, uncorrelated field a consumer would have to cross-reference `debug_snapshot()`'s own top-level `posture` key to find.
- **`next_planning_day`** - computed from a new `CATEGORY_INTERVAL` lookup table (`category -> its own tick interval`) plus the existing `_next_due_day()` function `debug_snapshot()` already trusted, now stamped onto the decision record itself rather than only available as a live, separately-queried value that could in principle disagree with what actually produced the record.
- **`rejected_candidates` entries** also gained a `targets` field (`_record_rejected_candidate()`'s own new optional parameter), for the same reason.

A new deterministic, checksummed counter, **`naval_ai_candidates_evaluated`**, closes the "candidates evaluated" bullet - distinct from the pre-existing `naval_ai_decisions` (which also counts non-candidate bookkeeping records like "fleet_sufficient" or "hold_stations," where there was nothing concrete to evaluate) and from `naval_ai_commands_submitted` (accepted only). It increments exactly once per concrete command candidate that was actually built and either accepted (`_submit()`'s success path) or rejected (`_record_rejected_candidate()`, reached both from `_submit()`'s own failure path and the four direct proactive-rejection call sites in `_plan_construction()`/`_consider_home_port()`/`_plan_transport()`).

**A deliberate scope boundary, stated precisely rather than overclaimed**: two existing rejection paths - `_plan_construction()`'s `insufficient_sailors` and `preserve_reserve` - are proactive resource pre-checks that run *before* any `ConstructShipCommand` object is even constructed, so they remain recorded only via `_record_decision()` (as before this packet) and do not bump `naval_ai_candidates_evaluated`. This mirrors the same distinction the counter draws everywhere else: a candidate is "evaluated" once a concrete command was actually built and assessed, not at every point the AI decided not to bother building one yet.

## Verification

- `tests/naval_ai_explainability_test.gd` (new): a posture decision's `posture`/`targets`/`constraints`/`next_planning_day` fields checked against the exact classification, treasury, and `_next_due_day()` calculation that produced them; a tactical decision's `targets` proven to include both the fleet and the zone it concerned; the `naval_ai_candidates_evaluated` counter proven to stay at zero for a pure bookkeeping decision, to increment by exactly one for a genuinely accepted candidate, and to increment by exactly one more (without touching `naval_ai_commands_submitted`) for a genuinely rejected one - using real `AssignAdmiralCommand` objects built and submitted through `_submit()`, not a synthetic counter increment.

## Deliberately out of scope for this packet

- **A structured `constraints` entry for every single decision point** - a handful of decisions genuinely have no threshold to name (e.g. "assign a free admiral to an unled fleet" - eligibility is binary, not a bound), and those correctly carry an empty dictionary rather than an invented one.
- **Bumping `naval_ai_candidates_evaluated` for proactive pre-checks that never reach command construction** (`insufficient_sailors`, `preserve_reserve`) - see "A deliberate scope boundary" above; a real, considered choice, not an oversight.
- **The four still-untested "Automated verification" roadmap claims** (same-zone player/AI battle arbitration, the full AI recovery matrix, trace-neutrality, a measured performance budget) - the closure audit's own recommended packet 6, not this one.
