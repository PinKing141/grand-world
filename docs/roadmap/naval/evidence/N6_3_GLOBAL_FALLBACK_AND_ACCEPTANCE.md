## Generic maritime AI fallback

06_N6's AI Content Rollout lists five steps: England/France, the Iberian slice, a Mediterranean fixture, "generic maritime fallback for all countries with eligible ports," and worldwide tuning last. `NavalAISystem` never needed a fourth step built separately - it was already generic by construction from N6.1 onward.

`NavalAISystem.process_day()` (`scripts/simulation/naval_ai_system.gd`) iterates `AIDefinitions.country_tags()` - the exact same roster `StrategicAISystem` (land AI) iterates. There is no naval-specific curated country list anywhere in the pillar, unlike the deliberately narrow Iberian vertical slice `AIDefinitions`/`assets/ai_definitions.json` still uses for AI enablement generally. The only naval-specific gate is `_is_maritime_capable(world, tag)`: does the country own at least one structurally real port (`MaritimeGraph.is_port_province`/`is_port_enabled`), independent of that port's content-review confidence. A country that is AI-enabled and owns a qualifying port receives full posture/construction/organisation/tactical/transport naval AI the day it first qualifies - no separate onboarding step, no per-country naval AI toggle to flip.

This was verified, not just asserted, by the existing `tests/naval_ai_test.gd` fixture: Castile, Aragon, and Portugal (real ports) build ships and make real decisions; Granada (harbour-level-0 "candidate" ports only) takes real, explained rejections rather than silently doing nothing; landlocked Navarre takes zero naval decisions, correctly, because it never reaches maritime-capable at all. None of those four outcomes required a single line of naval-specific per-country configuration - they fall out of the generic `_is_maritime_capable()` gate applied to whatever ports each country happens to own.

Worldwide tuning and balance (rollout step 5) is explicitly out of scope for this item, same as every other "not final balance content" caveat this pillar's ship/AI data already carries.

## Full-world fleet/ship/transport/battle/blockade stress

`tests/naval_battle_blockade_stress_smoke.gd` already covered four of the five mechanisms (fleet, ship, battle, blockade) across 29 real N0.3 fixture ports spanning several coastlines, from the N4.4/N5.3 UX/gate packet. It did not exercise transport operations at all - a real gap against N6.3's five-mechanism list, not just an unlabelled one.

The test now also creates one real transport operation per country that owns two or more fixture ports: an idle army and a `transport_cog`-crewed fleet at the country's first owned port, embarking via a genuine `CreateTransportOperationCommand` toward its second owned port, then ticked by the same `TransportSystem.process_day()` daily system N3 already proves correct in small, precise fixtures. Not every country is guaranteed a legal sea route between its two fixture ports (they were placed for coastline variety, not routing convenience), so the test counts what it actually manages (`transport_ops_created`) rather than asserting every multi-port country qualifies.

A real interaction was discovered while building this, not assumed: the transport fleet's origin and destination ports had to be reserved out of the battle-fleet setup loop. `NavalCombatSystem`'s own reinforcement rule correctly sweeps any co-located hostile fleet into a battle the moment one forms at that location - so placing the transport fleet at a port that also hosted the existing battle-pair fixture swept the transport fleet into that battle too, leaving `location_status` stuck at `BATTLE` and the embark timer permanently blocked (`_advance_embarking()` requires `DOCKED`). This is the same interaction the 100-seed acceptance test below hit independently and documented as "battle_paused is a real, undecided gap, not a bug" - accurate system behaviour, but one that defeats a test trying to isolate and prove transport progress specifically. The fix reserves the transport's two ports out of the battle/blockade fleet setup for that run, the same isolation strategy the 100-seed test settled on.

Every sea zone in `MaritimeGraph`, not just each port's immediate exit, is now registered as an unowned `province_states` entry before the run starts - a sailing transport fleet can legally pass through or end a day in any sea zone along its route, and the save validator rejects any fleet whose current location isn't a known province, the same rule that previously only needed each port's single real exit covered for blockaders.

Verified result after 20 concurrent simulated days: `ports=29 battle_fleets=18 blockade_fleets=9 battles=14 blockaded_provinces=5 transport_ops=10 transports_completed=3 transports_sailing_or_later=7`. All ten created transport operations show real progress (three fully completed and cleared from the registry, seven advanced past embarking into sailing or disembarking) rather than sitting untouched, alongside dense concurrent battle and blockade activity. A full save/load round trip afterward still passes every structural validator this pillar's own work touches (`_validate_naval_data`, `_validate_naval_battle_data`, the war-participants check, `_validate_transport_data`).

Countries remain 10 synthetic tags rather than the real political map - the same scope this stress test already carried before the transport extension, and a smoke-level capture rather than an approved N0 performance budget, per the test's own doc comment.

## 100-seed Channel acceptance report

`tests/naval_channel_100_seed_acceptance_test.gd` (new): 100 seeds (`BASE_SEED=14441111` plus seed index), each building a fresh England-vs-Burgundy war over the Channel and running three separate scenarios per seed:

- **Battle**: an asymmetric 4-vs-3 `war_galley` engagement at the Straits of Dover, 32 days. A prior symmetric 3-vs-3 draft always ground to the round cap with no meaningful variance across seeds - asymmetry lets per-round dice actually decide the outcome and round count.
- **Transport**: a real Kent-to-Calais `CreateTransportOperationCommand`, 15 days.
- **Blockade**: an England fleet assigned the `blockade` mission at the Straits of Dover via `SetFleetMissionCommand`, 3 days.

Each scenario runs in its own fresh `CampaignWorldState`, not one combined per-seed world - the same co-location/reinforcement discovery noted above: placing all three scenarios in the same sea zone let `NavalCombatSystem` correctly sweep the transport and blockade fleets into the battle as reinforcements, which is accurate behaviour but breaks a test that needs to isolate and count each mechanism independently.

Every scenario also runs `_check_structural_invariants()` (no duplicated or orphaned ships, no fleet in two active battles at once) and `_check_save_round_trip()` (save/load plus checksum match) before moving on.

Verified result across all 100 seeds: `seeds=100 battles_resolved=100 distinct_hull_outcomes=10 transports_completed=100 blockades_formed=100 elapsed_ms=113441.69`. Every seed's battle resolved, every transport completed, every blockade formed, and every structural/save check passed - and 10 distinct hull-loss outcomes across 100 seeds is the proof that these are genuinely different dice-driven runs, not one deterministic script repeated unchanged 100 times.

Registered in `tools/testing/run_all_tests.py` as a dedicated `TestSpec` (not the generic `GODOT_TESTS` tuple, whose default timeout is 120s) inside the `--quick`-excluded soak-test block, with `timeout=300` - the run measured ~113s in this environment, following the exact pattern the Phase 2/6/7/8 soak tests already established.

## Save migration and corruption suite

The "corruption" half of this item needed no new work. Every N-pillar test in this suite already asserts specific structural corruption is rejected at its own boundary: `_validate_naval_data` (orphan ships, non-reciprocal fleet/ship membership, duplicate ship listings, unknown owners - `naval_fleet_state_test.gd`), `_validate_naval_battle_data` (a fleet referencing an unknown battle - `naval_combat_test.gd`), `_validate_transport_data` (an operation referencing an unknown army, a dangling army-side reference - `naval_transport_operation_test.gd`), the blockaded-provinces reference check (`naval_blockade_test.gd`), and the war-participants check this session's own earlier peace/extinction cleanup packet fixed. Every stress and soak test in the suite also proves a save/load round trip stays clean at whatever scale it runs at. There was no real gap here to close.

The "migration" half had a genuine, specific gap. `CampaignWorldState.migrate_save_data()` cascades through eight schema steps (1 through 9), and six of those eight already had a dedicated test proving that exact adjacent-schema jump (`phase_3_movement_test.gd` for schema 1, `phase_4_economy_test.gd` for 2, `phase_7_character_test.gd` for 3, `phase_8_country_depth_test.gd` for 4, `naval_fleet_state_test.gd` for 5, `naval_transport_operation_test.gd` for 6). Nothing tested schema 7 (`naval_battle_registry`, added for N4A) or schema 8 (`blockaded_provinces`, added for N5.1) - the two schema bumps this pillar itself introduced - and nothing tested the full chain executing in one `migrate_save_data()` call rather than only in isolated single-step tests.

`tests/naval_save_schema_migration_test.gd` (new) closes this:

- Builds one real, populated fixture world - a war, a fleet/ship pair on each side of a genuine active naval battle with reciprocal `battle_id` references, and a blockaded province - rather than testing migration against an empty placeholder dict the way its content-blindness might otherwise let a test get away with.
- **Schema 7 -> current**: strips `naval_battle_registry` and `blockaded_provinces`, sets `schema_version` to 7, migrates, and confirms both registries come back as an explicit empty dict while the fixture's genuinely-present fleets, ships, and war survive untouched, `migrated_from_schema` correctly records `7`, and the result loads cleanly (once stale post-migration `battle_id` references on the surviving fleets - an artifact of this fixture's copy-then-strip construction, not something a real save/load path produces - are cleared, exactly as documented in the test's own comment).
- **Schema 8 -> current**: strips only `blockaded_provinces`, sets `schema_version` to 8, migrates, and confirms `blockaded_provinces` resets to empty while the already-present `naval_battle_registry` content is left untouched, and the result loads cleanly.
- **Full chain**: a genuinely ancient schema-1 dict (predating armies, economy, characters, country depth, and every naval registry entirely) run through one `migrate_save_data()` call reaches schema 9 in a single pass - `migrate_save_data()`'s cascading `if` (not `elif`) steps mean this was always structurally true, but nothing had proven it end-to-end before. Confirmed: a default army is recreated per owned country (schema 1->2's own rule), all seventeen later-added registries land as explicit empty dicts, and the fully-migrated result loads cleanly onto a fresh matching world.

Registered in `tools/testing/run_all_tests.py`'s `GODOT_TESTS` tuple (not the soak-test block - this test runs in well under a second).

## Deliberately simple / deferred

- The full-world stress test's ten synthetic countries are not the real political map - a genuine "every real AI-enabled country, every real port" full-world run is a larger undertaking than this item's own scope, and 06_N6's rollout step 5 ("worldwide tuning only after performance and historical content gates") explicitly defers it past this point anyway.
- The 100-seed acceptance report covers England/France only, per 06_N6's own rollout step 1 and this milestone's exit-gate wording ("complete the Channel construction/movement/transport/interception/battle/blockade/repair loop... complete 100 seeded repetitions"). The Iberian and Mediterranean fixtures already have their own correctness tests (`naval_ai_test.gd` etc.) but not a dedicated 100-seed acceptance run of their own - not required by the exit gate's own wording, which names the Channel specifically.
- `battle_paused` - holding a transport/blockade operation mid-execution while its own carrying fleet is swept into an unrelated battle, rather than only reacting after the fact - remains the same open, documented gap N3's own evidence already carries; this packet worked around it in both new tests rather than closing it, since closing it is N3/N4 scope, not N6.3's.
