# N5.2 - Repair and Construction Port Effects

**Status:** Recorded for the two effects with real mechanics to modify: repair rate reduction and construction delay. Sailor recovery reduction is not attempted - see below. Peace lifecycle and the trade-protection hook remain open.
**Satisfies:** the repair/construction/port-effects portion of [10 - Delivery sequence and checklist](../10_DELIVERY_SEQUENCE_AND_CHECKLIST.md) N5.2, and the remainder of the N5D work packet in [05 - N5 Strategic Effects](../05_N5_STRATEGIC_EFFECTS.md)
**Scope:** a blockade-aware repair rate multiplier in `FleetLogisticsSystem._process_repair()`, and a blockade-aware construction pause in `EconomySystem._complete_naval_construction()`. No new persistent state, no UI, no sailor-recovery change.

## Why sailor recovery reduction was not attempted

05_N5 "Province and Port Effects" lists "Optional sailor recovery reduction" alongside repair and construction effects - explicitly optional, unlike the other two. Sailor recovery (`SAILOR_RECOVERY_MONTHS`, in `EconomySystem.process_month()`) is computed as a single country-wide monthly aggregate against `maximum_sailors` (itself a sum of `SAILORS_PER_OWNED_PORT` across every enabled port the country owns) - there is no per-port sailor recovery figure to reduce. Attributing a reduction to "the blockaded fraction of a country's total sailor capacity" would require either splitting recovery per-port (a real redesign of an N2.2 mechanic this round's scope does not include) or an approximate country-wide formula that would not actually reflect which specific port is blockaded. Given the roadmap's own "optional" framing and the size of the redesign a correct version would need, this is left open rather than built as an approximation that would need redoing later.

## Architectural choices

**Repair rate is scaled by a flat multiplier once a docked fleet's own port clears a threshold, not blocked outright.** `_process_repair()` already gates repair behind `can_base()` (a stricter bar than "supplied") and a docked fleet - both already-correct gates this round leaves untouched. The new check computes `province_blockade_bp()` for the fleet's own `location_id` (a docked fleet's `location_id` *is* its port's province_id, no sea-zone translation needed) once per fleet per day, and multiplies `daily_rate_bp` by a flat `(10000 - BLOCKADE_REPAIR_PENALTY_BP)` fraction when the port's blockade meets `BLOCKADE_EFFECTIVENESS_THRESHOLD_BP` - the same threshold-then-flat-modifier shape `BlockadeSystem.siege_assist_bp()` already established, computed once outside the per-ship loop rather than once per ship, since it depends only on the fleet's location, not on any individual ship.

**Construction delay reuses the exact pause-one-day-at-a-time mechanism `_complete_naval_construction()` already has for ownership/control loss, rather than a new partial-progress formula.** Naval construction has no daily-accrual progress value the way a siege's `progress_bp` does - a project either completes on its precomputed `completion_day` or it doesn't. The existing ownership-loss branch already handles "this can't complete today" by pushing `completion_day` forward by exactly one day and re-checking tomorrow; a blockade above threshold is treated identically. This reads "reduced... effectiveness" as "zero progress on blockaded days" - the simplest faithful reading available given the existing all-or-nothing completion model, not a proportional slowdown.

**Each consumer keeps its own local threshold constant rather than sharing one from `BlockadeSystem`.** `FleetLogisticsSystem.BLOCKADE_EFFECTIVENESS_THRESHOLD_BP` and `EconomySystem.BLOCKADE_CONSTRUCTION_THRESHOLD_BP` both currently equal 5000 (the same value `BlockadeSystem.SIEGE_ASSIST_THRESHOLD_BP` also uses), but are three independent constants in three different files, matching this codebase's existing convention of every system owning its own placeholder magnitudes (each system already defines its own local `BASIS_POINTS` rather than importing a shared one) so any one of them can be tuned independently later without an unrelated consumer moving with it.

## What was built

- `scripts/simulation/fleet_logistics_system.gd`: `BlockadeSystemScript` preload; `BASIS_POINTS`; `BLOCKADE_EFFECTIVENESS_THRESHOLD_BP`/`BLOCKADE_REPAIR_PENALTY_BP` constants; `_process_repair()` computes a `rate_scale_bp` once per fleet from the port's own blockade level; `_repair_one_ship()` gained a `rate_scale_bp` parameter (default `BASIS_POINTS`, i.e. no change for every existing caller/behaviour) applied to `daily_rate_bp`.
- `scripts/simulation/economy_system.gd`: `BLOCKADE_CONSTRUCTION_THRESHOLD_BP` constant; `_complete_naval_construction()` gained a blockade check identical in shape to its existing ownership-loss pause, placed immediately after it.
- `tests/naval_blockade_test.gd`: extended (no new file) with a repair-rate section (two otherwise-identical damaged BUR fleets docked at Picardie, one with an above-threshold England blockade, proving the blockaded fleet's daily hull gain is exactly the expected reduced-rate value, not merely "less") and a construction-delay section (two otherwise-identical `ConstructShipCommand` projects at Picardie, forced to their completion day directly rather than simulated day-by-day - 150 days for a war galley - proving the unblockaded project completes and erases from the registry while the blockaded one persists with `completion_day` pushed forward by exactly one day).

## Results (verified via `naval_blockade_test.gd`, exit 0, no errors)

- An unblockaded port repairs a damaged ship at its full `repair_rate_bp` value from `assets/ship_definitions.json`, derived from the live ship definition rather than a hardcoded number.
- A port blockaded above threshold repairs the identical ship at exactly `repair_rate_bp * (10000 - BLOCKADE_REPAIR_PENALTY_BP) / 10000` - not merely "less," the precise formula value.
- An unblockaded port's naval construction, forced to its completion day, completes and is erased from `naval_construction_registry` in the same call.
- A blockaded port's identical construction, forced to the same completion day, is neither completed nor erased - its `completion_day` is pushed forward by exactly one day, mirroring the pre-existing ownership-loss pause byte-for-byte.
- No regression: re-ran all 42 Godot phase/core/naval tests after this round's changes - 41/42 pass, the one failure being the same pre-existing `country_label_layer_test.gd` frame-timing flake recorded in every N2/N3/N4/N5 evidence doc so far. `naval_fleet_logistics_test.gd` and `naval_economy_test.gd` both pass unaffected, confirming the new blockade checks are a no-op when no blockade is present.

## Deliberately simple / deferred

- **No sailor recovery reduction.** See the dedicated section above - the underlying mechanic is a country-wide aggregate, not per-port, and the roadmap marks this effect optional.
- **Construction delay is all-or-nothing per day, not proportional.** A port at exactly the threshold and a port at a full 10000 bp blockade both pause identically - simpler than a proportional slowdown, consistent with this round's other placeholder-first choices.
- **No event for repair/construction being actively suppressed by a blockade.** Neither change emits a new signal when the penalty/pause first takes effect - the existing `fleet_repair_progressed`/`naval_construction_completed` events simply fire less often (repair) or later (construction) than they otherwise would. A dedicated "port effectiveness reduced" signal is left to whichever slice eventually builds N5's still-open threshold-event infrastructure.
- **No naval-blockade-scale stress/performance test.** Both changes add one more `province_blockade_bp()` call per fleet-per-day (repair) or per-project-per-day (construction), on top of the already-deferred "full-coast calculation meets approved budget" item - still open, now with two more call sites depending on it.
