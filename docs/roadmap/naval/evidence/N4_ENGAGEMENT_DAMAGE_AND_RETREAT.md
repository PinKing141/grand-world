# N4 - Engagement, Damage, Sinking, and Forced Retreat

**Status:** Recorded - the first N4 round; enough for a naval battle to start and reach a terminal state. Reinforcement, positioning breakdown, morale, capture, voluntary retreat, and UX/gate are separate, explicitly open items.  
**Satisfies:** [10 - Delivery sequence and checklist](../10_DELIVERY_SEQUENCE_AND_CHECKLIST.md) N4.1 (partial), N4.2 (partial), N4.3 (partial), and the N4A/N4B work packets plus the essential parts of N4C in [04 - N4 Naval Combat](../04_N4_NAVAL_COMBAT.md)  
**Scope:** battle records, engagement start, deterministic hull damage, sinking, and forced retreat-or-destruction. Everything else 04_N4 describes - detection scoring, reinforcement, positioning breakdown, morale, capture, pursuit, voluntary retreat, peace/commander/lifecycle interaction, and all UX - is out of scope for this round.

## Why this round crosses the roadmap's own N4A/B/C boundary

04_N4's work packets split cleanly on paper: N4A is battle records and engagement start, N4B is positioning/damage/class roles, N4C is morale/sinking/capture/retreat/reinforcement. Stopping at N4A alone would mean nothing ever resolves a round. Stopping at N4A+N4B would mean ships take damage forever with no way to sink, retreat, or otherwise reach an end - a battle that starts and deals damage but can never conclude isn't a testable unit, it's an unfinished half-system left running. Sinking and retreat are technically listed under N4C, but they are the *minimum* needed for N4A+N4B to be a coherent, releasable slice at all - so this round pulls in exactly that minimum (sinking, forced retreat-or-destruction) while leaving the rest of N4C (capture, pursuit, reinforcement, morale-based early collapse) genuinely for later. This is the same reasoning that combined N2.2's economy-and-construction, and N3.2's embark-timing-and-sailing-and-disembark: a packet boundary that would ship an incomplete loop gets pulled forward just enough to close the loop, no further.

## Architectural choices

**`NavalCombatSystem` mirrors `WarfareSystem`'s land-combat architecture line for line, at fleet/ship scale.** Read first, then adapted: `_start_battles()`/`_resolve_battles()`/`_finish_battle()`/`_begin_retreat()` are structurally the same functions land combat already has, with province→sea-zone/port, army→fleet, and strength→hull substitutions. Damage formula shape (`power * (80 + roll*5) / 100`, a named per-battle RNG stream reused round to round, a terrain/zone modifier that only benefits the defending side) is copied from `WarfareSystem._resolve_battles()` exactly, not reinvented - the codebase already had one deterministic-combat pattern proven correct and tested; naval combat reuses it rather than inventing a second one that could quietly diverge.

**Naval battles get their own top-level `naval_battle_registry`, not nested inside `war_registry["battles"]` the way land battles are.** Every other naval concept this session built (fleets, ships, naval construction, transport operations) is a top-level registry with its own reverse indexes - a deliberate, consistent naval convention, not an oversight of land's different nested shape. `war_id` is still recorded on the battle record so war-score and future peace/reporting integration can find it.

**Completed battles are kept in the registry, not erased.** Transport operations and naval construction projects erase their own records on completion because they are transient bookkeeping with no post-completion value. A battle record is different: 04_N4 explicitly wants a "final report comparing starting/surviving/captured/sunk totals," which needs the record to still exist after the fight ends. This meant `_validate_naval_battle_data()` had to be written to only enforce live fleet-battle reciprocity for *active* battles - a completed battle's fleet list is a historical snapshot that may legitimately reference a fleet that has since left battle status, retreated elsewhere, or been destroyed and erased entirely.

**Ships fight at a fraction of their rating proportional to their own damage, and admiral martial skill applies once per fleet, not once per ship.** `_combat_power()` scales each ship's attack/defence contribution by its own `hull_bp`, so accumulated damage compounds within a single battle rather than only being a bookkeeping total. The admiral bonus (mirroring `WarfareSystem`'s commander-martial bonus exactly) is applied once per fleet represented among the ships being summed, not once per ship - an admiral commands a fleet, not a ship.

**A real, unrelated gap in `FleetMovementSystem` was found and fixed before retreat could work at all.** `FleetMovementSystem.advance_day()` only ever advanced fleets with `location_status == MOVING`; `ArmyMovementSystem`'s equivalent guard already included `RETREATING` alongside `MOVING` for land armies, but the fleet version never did, because nothing before this round ever set a fleet to `RETREATING` with a path to follow (the constant existed, unused, since N2.1). Fixed by widening the guard to match the army version. Without this fix, a retreating fleet would have been assigned a destination and a path and then simply never moved, forever - a real "no army/fleet may be permanently stranded" violation that this round's own retreat mechanism would have introduced if the underlying movement system hadn't been checked first.

## What was built

- `CampaignWorldState`: `naval_battle_registry` (new top-level registry), `make_naval_battle_record()`, `get_naval_battle()`, `_validate_naval_battle_data()` (structural/referential checks, active-battle-only reciprocity per the design choice above), `SAVE_SCHEMA_VERSION` 7→8 with a migration step.
- `scripts/simulation/naval_combat_system.gd` (new): `advance_day()`, `_start_battles()`, `_resolve_battles()`, `_finish_battle()`, `_begin_retreat()`, and the supporting `_living_fleets()`/`_combat_power()`/`_apply_hull_losses()`/`_remove_sunk_ships()` helpers.
- `fleet_movement_system.gd`: the `RETREATING` guard fix described above.
- Six new `SimulationEventBus` signals: `naval_battle_started`, `naval_battle_round_resolved`, `naval_battle_ended`, `ship_sunk`, `fleet_destroyed`, `fleet_retreat_started`.
- `simulation_controller.gd`: `NavalCombatSystem.advance_day` registered as a `daily_systems` entry, after `WarfareSystem`'s (mirroring land's own "movement, then combat" tick ordering).
- `tests/naval_combat_test.gd`, registered in `tools/testing/run_all_tests.py`.

## Results (verified via `naval_combat_test.gd`, exit 0, no errors)

- Two hostile fleets sharing a sea zone start a battle automatically; both sides correctly attributed to the war's attacker/defender per `DiplomacySystem.side_in_war`.
- A deliberately lopsided fight (3 war galleys vs. 1) reaches a terminal state well inside the 30-round cap, the stronger side wins, and war score moves in the attacker's favour by the same `war["battle_score_attacker"]` field land combat already uses.
- No ship exists in two fleets after the fight; every surviving ship's `fleet_id` still agrees with its fleet's own `ship_ids` list.
- Determinism: an identical fixture on an identical campaign seed resolves in the identical number of ticks, with the identical survivor and identical final hull.
- Retreat: a losing fleet (5 vs. 1, so the loser is clearly defeated but not annihilated) with a legal home port actually sails there over the following days and arrives - not frozen in `RETREATING` status, confirming the `FleetMovementSystem` fix works end-to-end, not just in isolation.
- Save/load mid-battle reproduces an identical checksum; a corrupted save with a fleet referencing an unknown battle is rejected.
- No regression: re-ran all 41 Godot phase/core/naval tests after this round's changes - 40/41 pass, the one failure being the same pre-existing `country_label_layer_test.gd` frame-timing flake recorded in every N2/N3 evidence doc so far.

## Bugs found and fixed before shipping (test, not implementation)

- The harness's `_require()`/`push_error()`/`quit(1)` pattern (documented as a pitfall multiple times earlier this session) masked four real assertion failures behind a final `quit(0)` that overrode them - a stark reminder to always scan full output text for `ERROR:` lines, never trust the printed "passed" message alone. Two of the four were genuine test-fixture bugs (a war record missing the `war_goal` field save validation requires; fleets placed at a sea zone absent from the lightweight test world's province map, which save validation correctly rejects as an unknown location - fixed by meeting at a real, owned port for the save/load-specific check instead). The other two surfaced the completed-battle-registry design decision described above.
- The determinism comparison originally ran a fixed 35 ticks for one world but an early-exit loop for the other, comparing `current_day` between runs that had stopped at different points for unrelated reasons - fixed by giving both worlds the identical early-exit condition.

## Deliberately simple / deferred

- **Detection/interception is a flat "always detect when co-located" rule.** 04_N4's scored detection (scouting, admiral manoeuvre, mission posture, seeded uncertainty) is real future work, not attempted here - the same "simple explainable formula first" precedent every other N2/N3 first slice already established.
- **No reinforcement.** A battle already in progress does not accept newly-arriving friendly fleets; they simply wait until the battle concludes, then can start a fresh one if hostiles remain.
- **No positioning breakdown, targeting by ship class/priority, or per-ship active-slot selection.** Combat power is a simple fleet-wide sum; 04_N4's richer positioning model (admiral manoeuvre, speed advantage, light-ship scouting, zone class, galley suitability, transport burden as distinct named modifiers with UI-visible sources) is future work.
- **No morale-based early collapse.** Ships have a `morale_contribution_bp` field, but nothing tracks or reduces battle morale yet; defeat is determined purely by hull (zero surviving ships, or the round cap with lower total hull losing).
- **No capture.** Ships only survive or sink in this slice - the explicit conditions, ownership transfer, and reserve-fleet handling 04_N4 describes for capture are not built.
- **No voluntary retreat.** Only forced retreat-on-defeat exists; there is no player/AI-requested mid-battle retreat command, minimum-duration rule, or pursuit-damage step.
- **No peace/commander/country-lifecycle interaction during an active battle.** Consistent with the pre-existing extinction-cleanup gap N3.3 already documented for armies/fleets in general.
- **No UX.** No battle marker, battle panel, final report, or retreat control - `NavalHUD`/`campaign_interface_shell.gd` do not yet expose naval battles at all. This is N4.4's job, mirroring how N2.5/N3.4 were their pillars' dedicated UX slices.
- **No naval-battle-scale stress/performance test.** N2.5's stress smoke covers fleet logistics at scale; nothing yet constructs many simultaneous naval battles and measures `NavalCombatSystem`'s daily cost at that scale.
- **The "no legal retreat → explicit destruction" path is unit-correct but not exercised end-to-end.** `_begin_retreat()`'s destruction branch mirrors `TransportSystem._destroy_stranded_operation()`'s already-proven erase-and-emit pattern exactly. Constructing a genuine "no port anywhere on the real map is reachable" scenario is impractical in a lightweight test fixture for the identical reason N3.3's evidence doc already recorded: any province absent from a sparse fixture's ownership map reads as "unclaimed," and `NavalAccessPolicy.can_dock` treats an unclaimed port as dockable by anyone.
