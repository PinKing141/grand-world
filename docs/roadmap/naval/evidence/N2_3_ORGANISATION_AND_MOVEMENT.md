# N2.3 - Fleet Organisation and Movement

**Status:** Recorded  
**Satisfies:** [10 - Delivery sequence and checklist](../10_DELIVERY_SEQUENCE_AND_CHECKLIST.md) N2.3, and the N2B work packet in [02 - N2 Fleet Logistics](../02_N2_FLEET_LOGISTICS.md)  
**Scope:** create/split/transfer/merge/home-port commands, fleet aggregates, and point-to-point movement with cancellation and mid-route access revalidation. No basing/supply status, attrition, repair, or admirals - those are N2.4.

## Architectural choices

**`MaritimeGraph` stays access-blind; `NavalAccessPolicy` gained the route layer.** Legal-route finding (`can I actually get there, given who I am`) needed to reuse `MaritimeGraph`'s Dijkstra core without teaching the topology layer about ownership. `MaritimeGraph.find_route()`/`_dijkstra_from()` gained an optional `port_filter: Callable` parameter - called with a port ID whenever the route would pass through or arrive at it, must return `true` to allow it - and `NavalAccessPolicy.find_legal_route()` wraps this with `func(port_id): return can_dock(...)`. This keeps the class boundary identical to the pre-existing `ProvinceGraph`/`ProvincePathfinder` split rather than inventing a new layering rule.

**One mutation primitive for all four organisation commands.** `CreateFleetCommand`, `SplitFleetCommand`, `TransferShipsCommand`, and `MergeFleetsCommand` all reduce to "take this set of ship IDs and put them in that fleet" - so they share `FleetSystem.move_ships()` as the single mutation path, and `FleetSystem.shared_organisable_port()` as the single eligibility check (returns `-1` if the ships aren't all docked at the same port under the issuing country's control). This avoids four near-duplicate validate/apply implementations that could quietly drift apart.

**Movement mirrors `ArmyMovementSystem` exactly, including its timing model.** `FleetMovementSystem.advance_day()` is a line-for-line structural match: a `next_arrival_day` compared against `world.current_day` *before* the scheduler increments the day counter (`SimulationScheduler.advance_one_day()` runs `daily_systems` first, then increments `current_day`). This means a 1-day leg only becomes visible on the *second* `advance_one_day()` call after it was queued, not the first - a non-obvious consequence of "commands, then daily rules, then the day advances" that the test had to be corrected against once discovered (see Bugs found below). Each leg is revalidated at entry time, not just at `MoveFleetCommand` submission, so a mid-route capture halts the fleet at its last legal node rather than teleporting past the loss or silently completing into hostile water.

**Fleet aggregates are recomputed everywhere membership can change, not just where the command was named for it.** `FleetSystem.recompute_aggregate()` is called from the 4 organisation commands (via `move_ships()`) and from `EconomySystem._complete_naval_construction()` - the latter was a gap left over from N2.2, where a freshly completed ship joined its port reserve fleet without the aggregate being recomputed. Closed as part of this slice so "no authoritative aggregate may disagree with underlying ships" ([00 - Scope](../00_SCOPE_AND_ARCHITECTURE_LOCK.md)) holds for every membership-changing path, not just the new ones.

**Organisation is deliberately scoped to same-port, all-docked fleets.** Merging/splitting/transferring ships that are at sea, mid-movement, in battle, or retreating is out of scope for this slice - `FleetSystem.is_docked_and_organisable()` enforces it. Documented as a first-slice simplification in `fleet_system.gd`'s class comment, not an oversight; revisiting it is not currently planned work.

## What was built

- `scripts/simulation/fleet_system.gd` (new): `speed_multiplier_bp()`, `recompute_aggregate()`, `is_docked_and_organisable()`, `shared_organisable_port()`, `move_ships()`.
- Five organisation commands (new): `create_fleet_command.gd`, `split_fleet_command.gd`, `transfer_ships_command.gd`, `merge_fleets_command.gd` (merges into the lowest-sorted fleet ID among those named, for determinism), `set_fleet_home_port_command.gd` (validated via `NavalAccessPolicy.can_base`, the basing-rights question, not merely `can_dock`).
- Two movement commands (new): `move_fleet_command.gd`, `cancel_fleet_movement_command.gd`.
- `scripts/simulation/fleet_movement_system.gd` (new): `advance_day()`, `_entry_failure_reason()`, `_update_progress()`, `_finish()` - structurally mirrors `army_movement_system.gd`.
- `CampaignWorldState.make_fleet_record()` extended with an `"aggregate"` sub-dict (`ship_count`, `total_hull`, `total_maximum_hull`, `total_attack`, `total_defence`, `total_blockade_power`, `total_transport_capacity`, `speed`).
- `MaritimeGraph.find_route()`/`_dijkstra_from()` extended with the `port_filter: Callable` parameter; `NavalAccessPolicy.find_legal_route()` added.
- Eight new `SimulationEventBus` signals: `fleet_created`, `fleet_ships_transferred`, `fleets_merged`, `fleet_home_port_changed`, `fleet_movement_ordered`, `fleet_moved`, `fleet_movement_completed`, `fleet_movement_blocked`, `fleet_movement_cancelled`.
- `EconomySystem._complete_naval_construction()` updated to call `FleetSystem.recompute_aggregate()` (closing the N2.2 gap noted above).
- `tests/naval_fleet_organisation_test.gd` and `tests/naval_fleet_movement_test.gd`, both registered in `tools/testing/run_all_tests.py`.

## Results (verified via both new tests, exit 0, no errors)

- `naval_fleet_organisation_test.gd`: create pulls ships from two co-located fleets into a new fleet and erases any source fleet left empty; split peels a ship back out into its own fleet; transfer moves a ship into an existing fleet and erases the now-empty source; merge consolidates every named fleet into the lowest-sorted fleet ID. Across all four operations, `country_ships("ENG").size()` stays exactly 4 - no ship is ever lost or duplicated. Aggregates are checked directly (e.g. a fleet's `speed` equals its slowest ship, `war_galley`'s 4, not the faster `light_caravel`'s 6). Rejections verified: empty ship list, ships at different ports, a country that doesn't own the ship, merging fewer than two fleets, merging fleets at different ports.
- `naval_fleet_movement_test.gd`: exercises the fixture Channel crossing (Calais port 87 → Straits of Dover sea zone 1271 → Kent port 235, established in N0/N1). Confirms: rejections (moving to the current location, to an unrelated foreign port, by a country that doesn't own the fleet); the happy path takes exactly 2 one-day legs to complete, observable after 3 `advance_one_day()` ticks given the check-before-increment timing described above; a mid-route capture of the destination (both `set_province_owner` *and* `set_province_controller`, since `NavalAccessPolicy` prefers controller over owner) halts the fleet at the Straits of Dover in `at_sea` status with no pending arrival, rather than completing into now-hostile Kent; `CancelFleetMovementCommand` mid-route leaves the fleet `at_sea` at its current node with an empty remaining path, and is rejected against an already-stationary fleet; an identical order replayed in a fresh world resolves in the identical number of ticks to the identical `docked` end state.
- No regression: re-ran all 33 Godot phase/core/naval tests registered in `tools/testing/run_all_tests.py` (`country_registry_test` through this slice's two new tests) after this round of `fleet_system.gd`/`fleet_movement_system.gd`/`maritime_graph.gd`/`economy_system.gd` changes - 32/33 pass. The one failure, `country_label_layer_test.gd`, is a frame-timing budget check (703-country label layout must stay under a 30 ms/1000 ms budget) that fails identically in isolation with no other process running; it touches only label-rendering code this slice never modified, and its numbers point to this machine simply being slower than whatever baseline the budget was tuned against - a pre-existing, naval-unrelated flake, not a regression from this work.

## Bugs found and fixed (during this slice, before the tests were accepted)

- **Test fixture bug, not a code bug:** `naval_fleet_movement_test.gd` initially asserted a 1-day leg would be visible after a *single* `advance_one_day()` call. `FleetMovementSystem.advance_day()` compares `world.current_day < next_arrival_day` *before* `SimulationScheduler.advance_one_day()` increments the day counter (mirroring `ArmyMovementSystem` exactly), so a 1-day leg queued on day D only resolves on the tick that begins with `current_day == D+1` - i.e. the *second* `advance_one_day()` call after the leg was queued, not the first. Fixed by correcting the test's tick counts (2 calls to reach the Straits of Dover, 3 to reach Kent) rather than changing the simulation's timing model, since the model already matches the army equivalent and changing it would put fleets and armies out of sync.
- **Test fixture bug, not a code bug:** the mid-route capture scenario originally called only `world.set_province_owner()`. `NavalAccessPolicy._port_host()` prefers `controller` over `owner` when both are set (deliberately, so occupation - not just formal ownership - governs docking rights). Since `set_province_owner()` does not touch `controller`, the fixture's "captured" port still resolved to its original controller and the block never triggered. Fixed by also calling `world.set_province_controller()`, matching what a real invasion/occupation would do to both fields.
- Also carried forward from N2.2: `EconomySystem._complete_naval_construction()` was not calling `FleetSystem.recompute_aggregate()` on the newly created ship's fleet (a gap since `FleetSystem` didn't exist yet at N2.2 time). Closed in this slice; see architectural choices above.

## Deliberately simple / deferred

- Organisation commands require every named fleet/ship to be `docked` at the same port - no organising a fleet at sea, mid-movement, in battle, or retreating. Not currently planned to change.
- No basing/supply status, attrition, or repair for a fleet sitting idle at sea or in hostile waters - N2.4.
- No admiral assignment or lifecycle - N2.4.
- No blockade, interception, or combat trigger on `fleet_movement_blocked`/mid-route encounters - that is N4's concern; this slice only guarantees a blocked fleet halts safely.
