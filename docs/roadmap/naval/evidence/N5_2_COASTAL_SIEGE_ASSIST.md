# N5.2 - Coastal Siege Assist

**Status:** Recorded for the one effect with a real target to modify: daily siege progress bonus. "Removal/reduction of a coastal resupply penalty" and "garrison recovery reduction" are not attempted - see below. Repair/construction/port effects, peace lifecycle, and the trade-protection hook remain open.
**Satisfies:** the coastal-siege-contribution portion of [10 - Delivery sequence and checklist](../10_DELIVERY_SEQUENCE_AND_CHECKLIST.md) N5.2, and the N5C work packet in [05 - N5 Strategic Effects](../05_N5_STRATEGIC_EFFECTS.md)
**Scope:** a side-scoped blockade query (`BlockadeSystem.blockade_bp_by_side()`/`siege_assist_bp()`) and a single call site in `WarfareSystem._advance_sieges_and_occupations()` that applies a flat daily-progress bonus when the besieging side's own blockade of the target port clears a configured threshold. No new persistent state, no siege-authority change, no UI.

## Why this is a small, single-effect packet

05_N5 "Coastal Siege Support" lists three "possible bounded effects": a daily siege progress bonus, removal/reduction of a coastal resupply penalty, and garrison recovery reduction "if that mechanic exists." A direct search of `warfare_system.gd` found no coastal resupply penalty anywhere in land warfare, and the siege dict's `garrison` field (`"garrison": 500`) is set once at siege creation and never read or modified anywhere else in the file - there is no garrison recovery mechanic to reduce. Both of those effects are consequently inapplicable today, not deferred - there is nothing for a naval blockade to modify until land warfare grows a resupply or garrison-recovery mechanic of its own. That leaves exactly one real target: `_advance_sieges_and_occupations()`'s `daily_progress` computation, which already exists and already has a fort-level modifier sitting right next to where a blockade modifier belongs.

## Architectural choices

**Blockade assist is side-scoped, not world-scoped.** `province_blockade_bp()` (N5.1/N5.1-target-resistance) sums power from *every* hostile eligible fleet blockading a province, regardless of which specific war or war side owns that fleet - correct for the ledger and war-score consumers, which only care about "is this province blockaded at all." A coastal siege has a specific besieging country and a specific war; crediting it with a blockade contributed by an unrelated ally's fleet, or a fleet from an entirely different war the same two countries happen to also be fighting, would be wrong. `blockade_bp_by_side()` (built on a new shared `_sum_eligible_power()` helper `province_blockade_bp()` was refactored to reuse) restricts the summation to a given list of countries - the besieging war side - before applying the same resistance-adjusted bp formula.

**The coastal-linkage gate reuses `NavalDefinitions.is_port()`, not "is this province a `land_neighbors()` result of some sea zone."** 05_N5 requires the province be "genuinely coastal and linked to the blockading zone" before a siege can be assisted. `blockaded_provinces_for_fleet()` already answers a looser version of that question (any land neighbour of the fleet's sea zone, whether or not it is a registered port), which is intentionally permissive for blockade *targeting* - a fleet can blockade a stretch of undeveloped coastline, not just a named port. Siege assist is stricter: `siege_assist_bp()` requires the target to actually be a registered port with real port data (the same `is_port()` check target resistance's `harbour_level` lookup already uses), which is a tighter and more defensible reading of "genuinely coastal and linked" for a mechanic that specifically boosts a *siege* - land sieges of undeveloped, non-port coastline are not naval-assistable by this rule.

**The threshold and bonus are placeholder constants, not approved balance, chosen for the same "simplest explicit rule" reason every prior first-slice formula in this codebase used.** `SIEGE_ASSIST_THRESHOLD_BP = 5000` (a blockade must reach at least half effectiveness before it counts) and `SIEGE_ASSIST_BONUS_BP = 2500` (a flat 25% daily-progress speedup, applied once as a multiplier, not stacked or scaled by blockade strength above the threshold) are the simplest binary-threshold, flat-bonus shapes consistent with `DAMAGED_EFFECTIVENESS_THRESHOLD_BP`'s existing precedent elsewhere in `BlockadeSystem`.

**The "port not already controlled in a way that makes blockade irrelevant" condition needed no new code.** `_advance_sieges_and_occupations()` already has `if current_controller == besieger_country: continue` immediately before siege creation/update - a besieger already controlling the province never gets (or keeps) a siege entry at all, naval-assisted or not. This was true before this round and remains the correct gate.

**Naval only publishes a query; `WarfareSystem` remains siege authority.** `siege_assist_bp()` returns an integer bp value or zero - it does not touch `war["sieges"]`, `province_states`, or any occupation/controller state. `_advance_sieges_and_occupations()` is the only caller and the only place that turns the returned value into an actual `daily_progress` change, matching 05_N5's explicit "the land warfare system remains siege authority. Naval publishes a blockade contribution query; it does not directly complete or occupy provinces."

## What was built

- `scripts/simulation/blockade_system.gd`: `_sum_eligible_power()` (shared power-summation helper, factored out of `province_blockade_bp()` so `blockade_bp_by_side()` doesn't duplicate the fleet-scan loop); `blockade_bp_by_side()`; `SIEGE_ASSIST_THRESHOLD_BP`/`SIEGE_ASSIST_BONUS_BP` constants; `siege_assist_bp()`.
- `scripts/simulation/warfare_system.gd`: `_advance_sieges_and_occupations()` now resolves the besieging side's country list from the war record and applies `siege_assist_bp()`'s flat bonus to `daily_progress` when it clears the threshold. New `BlockadeSystemScript` preload.
- `tests/naval_blockade_test.gd`: extended (no new file) with a coastal-siege-assist section - two parallel worlds (identical war/army/economy fixture, one with an above-threshold blockading fleet, one without) proving assisted siege progress exceeds unassisted progress after an identical number of days, plus threshold and side-scoping checks.

## A real fixture bug found and fixed while writing this test

`CampaignWorldState.initialize()` auto-creates one default army per country at that country's lowest-ID province (`_create_default_armies()` - a pre-existing, unrelated Phase 3 test-setup convenience, not something this round added). `naval_blockade_test.gd`'s fixture gives Burgundy only Picardie, so a default `"a_BUR"` army already sits there before any test code runs. The first version of the new siege-assist test placed an England army directly at Picardie expecting an uncontested siege; instead it collided with this pre-existing defender and started a land battle (visible via `world.war_registry["war_1"]["battles"]` populating with an active battle instead of `sieges`), so the England army's status flipped to `ARMY_STATUS_BATTLE` and `_advance_sieges_and_occupations()`'s battle/retreating exclusion correctly, silently skipped it as a besieger candidate - the siege dict simply never gained a Picardie entry. Root-caused via a standalone debug probe script that printed the actual post-`advance_day()` war state rather than continuing to reason about the code in the abstract. Fixed by erasing `"a_BUR"` from both comparison worlds' `army_registry` before placing the siege army, isolating the test to siege-assist behaviour specifically rather than re-covering the already-tested battle-to-siege transition `phase_5_warfare_test.gd` exercises elsewhere.

A second, purely mechanical bug surfaced during the same debugging: bracket-indexing a GDScript `Dictionary` with a key that does not exist raises a runtime `SCRIPT ERROR` in this Godot version rather than returning `null` - and, per this session's already-documented harness quirk, an uncaught script error aborts `_run()` without reaching `quit()`, leaving the headless process running indefinitely rather than failing fast. Two lines that used `dict[key]` where the key's presence hadn't yet been established were rewritten to `dict.get(key, default)` (or an explicit `.has()` check first), and every process left running by this discovery was killed manually before it consumed further test-run time.

## Results (verified via `naval_blockade_test.gd`, exit 0, no errors)

- A blockade below the configured threshold (the fixture's earlier 5-power-vs-22-required-power combination, ~23% blockade) grants zero siege assist.
- `blockade_bp_by_side()` restricted to a coalition that does not own the blockading fleet (`["BUR"]`, when the fleet is England's) correctly returns zero - it does not fall back to crediting the whole world's hostile presence.
- Two otherwise-identical sieges (same war, same province, same start day, same weekly random-bonus draws since both worlds share the same RNG stream key and call order) diverge only by the assisted world's flat daily-progress multiplier; after 5 simulated days the assisted siege's `progress_bp` is strictly greater than the unassisted siege's.
- No regression: re-ran all 42 Godot phase/core/naval tests after this round's changes - 41/42 pass, the one failure being the same pre-existing `country_label_layer_test.gd` frame-timing flake recorded in every N2/N3/N4/N5 evidence doc so far. `phase_5_warfare_test.gd`'s own full battle-to-siege-to-occupation loop passes unaffected, confirming the new `BlockadeSystemScript` call in `_advance_sieges_and_occupations()` is a no-op when no naval blockade is present.

## Deliberately simple / deferred

- **No coastal resupply penalty reduction or garrison recovery reduction.** Neither underlying land-warfare mechanic exists yet (see above) - not a scope cut, there is genuinely nothing to modify.
- **No "coastal siege support changed" event.** *(Built in a later round - see [N5_2_COASTAL_SIEGE_SUPPORT_CHANGED_EVENT.md](N5_2_COASTAL_SIEGE_SUPPORT_CHANGED_EVENT.md), which adds a `blockade_assisted` flag directly to each siege record to detect the transition, exactly the "needs a persisted previous value" fix anticipated here.)*
- **The assist bonus is a flat multiplier, not scaled by how far above the threshold the blockade sits.** A blockade at exactly 5000 bp and one at 10000 bp grant the identical 25% bonus - simpler than a proportional formula, and consistent with this session's placeholder-first precedent, but not what a more granular reading of "possible bounded effects" might eventually want.
- **No interaction with the still-open Contested Zones item.** If an enemy fleet were present and eligible to blockade back, `province_blockade_bp()`/`blockade_bp_by_side()` do not yet reduce a side's contribution for that - unchanged from N5.1/target-resistance's existing scope.
- **No naval-siege-assist-scale stress/performance test.** `siege_assist_bp()` adds one more `NavalDefinitions.load_default()` read plus a full `blockade_bp_by_side()` fleet scan per besieged province per day - on top of the already-deferred "full-coast calculation meets approved budget" item, now with a third call site depending on it.
