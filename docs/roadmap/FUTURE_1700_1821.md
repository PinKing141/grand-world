# Future Expansion — 1700 to 1821

## Status

Deferred. This is not part of the initial 1444–1700 production scope.

## Entry Gate

Work begins only after:

- The 1444–1700 campaign is released or reaches an explicitly approved post-release stability gate.
- Long campaigns are performant.
- Save migrations are reliable.
- Technology and government systems can support new eras.
- Existing content quality is not being sacrificed to extend the timeline.

## Expansion Themes

- Enlightenment.
- Constitutionalism.
- Absolutism and its limits.
- Revolutionary politics.
- Nationalism.
- Independence movements.
- Expanded colonial conflict.
- Early industrialisation.
- Mass conscription.
- Larger professional armies.
- Global imperial warfare.

## Required System Extensions

### Government and Politics

- Constitutional monarchies.
- Republic variants.
- Revolutionary governments.
- Political reform.
- Expanded legitimacy or authority models.

### Technology

- Late military formations.
- Improved artillery.
- Naval technology.
- Administrative bureaucracy.
- Early industrial production.

### Population and Society

- Urbanisation.
- Literacy.
- Political ideology.
- National identity.
- Revolutionary unrest.

### Economy

- Manufacturing expansion.
- Early industrial buildings.
- Expanded global markets.
- State debt and finance.

### Warfare

- Mass mobilisation.
- Larger armies.
- Attrition and supply refinement.
- Revolutionary warfare.
- Independence wars.

### Diplomacy

- Great-power balance.
- Revolutionary coalitions.
- Independence guarantees.
- Expanded subject and colonial diplomacy.

## Save Compatibility

The 1444–1700 save model should already store:

- Integer campaign date.
- Versioned technology.
- Versioned government.
- Data-driven units and buildings.
- Event and modifier definitions.
- Country, province, character, and title state.

The extension should add definitions and migrations, not replace the core simulation architecture.

## Risks

- The era requires population and political systems deeper than the base game.
- Extending the timeline may expose severe late-game performance issues.
- Revolutionary and national systems may not fit purely dynastic mechanics.
- Content volume may be comparable to another full production cycle.

## Explicit Rule

Do not pull 1700–1821 systems into the active 1444–1700 roadmap unless they are necessary architectural foundations and do not require era-specific content or gameplay.
