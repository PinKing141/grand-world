# MV-0 Scale, Projection, and Realm-Presentation Audit

## Decision summary

France's province geometry is not uniquely undersized. Its apparent thinness comes primarily from non-equivalent camera framing and the absence of starting appanage relationships, with border weight and colour noise amplifying the effect.

MV-0 therefore forbids isolated geographic scaling of France or its provinces. The approved response is:

1. compare regions at matched screen coverage;
2. use an atlas-like orthographic projection at strategic and regional zooms;
3. retain separate legal ownership for every appanage;
4. initialize historical subject metadata in the 1444 scenario;
5. add subject cues through presentation rules, never by rewriting ownership;
6. revisit regional exaggeration only as a versioned whole-map projection/topology project.

## Evidence

The authoritative province image is `5632×2048` and maps to a `56.32×20.48` world plane. It contains approximately 4,941 province-definition rows.

| Country | Direct provinces | Source-pixel area | Mean province area |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| France | 20 | 6,047 | 302 |
| England | 40 | 9,421 | 236 |
| Castile | 33 | 10,974 | 333 |
| Aragon | 25 | 4,859 | 194 |
| Burgundy | 11 | 2,554 | 232 |
| Brittany | 5 | 1,267 | 253 |
| Orléans | 4 | 1,048 | 262 |

French provinces are therefore larger on average than English, Burgundian, Breton, Aragonese, and Orléanais provinces. Direct France is the small political shape—not a uniquely low-resolution French province set.

The reviewed French realm includes 20 directly owned provinces plus four Orléanais, two Bourbonnais, one Auvergnat, and two Foix provinces. That is 29 provinces and approximately 8,552 source pixels when the subject realm is considered, while the legal owners remain unchanged.

## Camera comparison

The reproducible comparison is generated by:

~~~powershell
python tools/map_visual_audit/capture_mv0_camera_comparison.py
~~~

Both captures use `1920×1080`, the same France focus, the same camera transform, and a matched `2.4044` world-unit vertical map span.

| Profile | Projection | Presentation | Motion P95 on AMD Radeon 610M |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Existing | Perspective, 75° FOV, 15° from vertical | Near/far scale drift and foreshortening | 30.995 ms |
| Candidate | Orthographic, matched size `2.3225` | Stable atlas scale and regional silhouette | 29.647 ms |

These are diagnostic results after unreadably small labels were culled, not final shipping performance. Perspective rendered 55 country labels in 200 draws; orthographic rendered 62 in 208 draws because its horizontal footprint exposes a slightly different set. The cull substantially improved the earlier 344–352-draw comparison, but an external GPU capture and the batched label renderer remain required.

### Approved projection policy

- **Strategic and regional zoom:** orthographic.
- **Close geographic/object zoom:** optional gentle perspective only if the transition passes scale, selection, label, and motion tests.
- **Default perspective FOV:** the engine's inherited 75° is rejected as the final strategic-map default.
- **Comparison rule:** references must be matched by geographic bounds and screen resolution, not compared against a full-world screenshot.
- **Implementation location:** MV1-02 owns the runtime projection/transition service. MV-0 owns this decision and its reproducible evidence.

## 1444 France/appanage rules

The scenario now initializes France as overlord of:

- Auvergne (`AUV`);
- Bourbonnais (`BOU`);
- Foix (`FOI`);
- Orléans (`ORL`).

Each record uses simulation type `vassal` and presentation type `appanage`. Brittany (`BRI`) and Provence (`PRO`) remain outside this starting subject list.

### Default political ownership mode

- Every appanage retains its owner colour and sovereign/province topology.
- France's fill and normal country label use directly owned French components only.
- Appanage borders receive a relationship treatment that is quieter than a fully foreign sovereign edge but clearer than an internal province edge.
- No French fill or label may cross appanage land merely to make France look larger.

### Realm/diplomatic mode

- France and its subjects may share a restrained tint, hatch family, outline, or realm halo.
- The relationship is derived from `subject_registry.presentation`, not tag-specific shader branches.
- A realm label may span the realm only in an explicitly named realm/diplomatic mode.
- Tooltips and country panels must preserve the difference between owner, controller, subject, and overlord.

### Ownership transitions

Annexation, release, integration, subject independence, formation, and save/load must invalidate realm cues and labels. Presentation never mutates province ownership as a side effect.

## Why isolated land enlargement is rejected

Scaling France alone would desynchronize adjacent borders, terrain, height, biomes, water, selection, travel distance, rivers, labels, and every derived bake. Enlarging individual provinces would require merging, redrawing, or a versioned projection transformation.

If playtesting later proves that Europe needs deliberate exaggeration, create a map-foundation project with:

1. a documented project-owned projection;
2. stable-ID migration rules;
3. regenerated province, terrain, water, biome, height, river, coast, and label assets;
4. adjacency/pathfinding and save migrations;
5. global rather than country-specific geographic review.

## Acceptance gates

- The Phase 8 integration test proves the four starting appanages and excludes Brittany/Provence.
- Province ownership remains separate before and after save/load.
- Camera A/B captures match vertical coverage within `0.02` world units.
- Art/UX review accepts the orthographic regional silhouette.
- The future subject border treatment passes France/Low Countries, Holy Roman Empire, personal-union, and colonial-subject fixtures.
- No shipped mode labels France across subject land without clearly declaring realm semantics.
